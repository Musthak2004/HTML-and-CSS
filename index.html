Mythical creatures

Dragon
A dragon is a mythical creature found in the folklore of cultures throughout the world. It is roughly serpentine in form, and often possesses features drawn from different animals, such as wings and claws. In nearly all cultures, it is conceived of as a powerful and awe-inspiring creature.

Phoenix
A Phoenix Dragon is a mythical hybrid combining the immense strength and fiery nature of a dragon with the regal, immortal, and regenerative qualities of a phoenix. Often depicted with vibrant red and yellow plumage, these creatures represent a fusion of elemental power (fire) and the cycle of rebirth from ashes.

Vampire
A vampire is a legendary undead creature that feeds on the vital essence, typically blood, of the living, commonly depicted with fangs, pale skin, and aversion to sunlight. Originating from folklore in Eastern Europe and the Balkans, they were believed to be corpses that returned to haunt loved ones.

Werewolf
A werewolf (or lycanthrope) is a mythical figure, rooted in ancient European folklore and Greek mythology (e.g., Lycaon), depicting a human who transforms into a wolf or hybrid creature, usually during a full moon. Often cursed or bitten, they are portrayed as ravenous, nearly invulnerable, and vulnerable only to silver.
